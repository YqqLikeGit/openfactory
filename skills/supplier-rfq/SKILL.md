# Supplier RFQ — get quotes that mean something

Factories ignore vague inquiries because quoting costs them engineering hours. A good
RFQ signals "this buyer is real and knows what they need" — it gets faster, tighter,
more honest numbers. That signal is the whole game.

## What every RFQ must contain (the factory's checklist)

1. **What**: product name + one-line description, drawings/CAD/photos attached.
   No drawing? A dimensioned sketch and reference product links beat paragraphs.
2. **Material & finish**: resin/alloy grade, color (Pantone), texture/polish, logo method
   (silkscreen / laser / in-mold). "Same as attached sample" is acceptable and respected.
3. **Quantity tiers**: ask for 3 price points, e.g. 1k / 5k / 20k. Single-quantity RFQs
   get single-use prices.
4. **Quality/compliance bar**: certifications needed (CE/FCC/FDA/LFGB/EAC…), inspection
   standard (e.g. AQL 2.5), any critical dimensions.
5. **Packaging**: bulk, polybag, or retail box (who designs the box?).
6. **Terms asked**: FOB port?, sample cost + lead time, tooling cost + ownership,
   production lead time, payment terms.
7. **Who you are**: company, market, realistic annual volume. Factories quote serious
   buyers differently — one honest sentence here changes the number.

## Template (English)

> Subject: RFQ — [product] — [3 qty tiers] — [target ship month]
>
> Hi [name], we are [company], selling [product category] in [market].
> Please quote the attached [product]:
> - Material/finish: […]  - Quantities: [1k / 5k / 20k]
> - Certifications: […]   - Packaging: […]
> Please include: unit price per tier (FOB [port]), tooling cost & ownership terms,
> sample cost & lead time, production lead time, and payment terms.
> Target: samples by [date]. We're comparing [2–3] suppliers this round.

## 模板（中文）

> 主题：询价——[产品]——[三档数量]——[目标出货月份]
>
> [称呼]您好，我们是[公司]，主营[品类]，销往[市场]。请对附件产品报价：
> 材质/表面处理：[…]；数量：[1k/5k/2万]；认证要求：[…]；包装：[…]。
> 请报：各档 FOB [港口] 单价、模具费及模具归属、样品费用与周期、量产周期、付款方式。
> 目标[日期]前拿到样品。本轮同时对比 2–3 家供应商。

## Reading the replies — signal table

| Reply behavior | Read it as |
|---|---|
| Asks sharp questions about your drawing before quoting | Engineer touched it — promote this supplier |
| Instant price with no questions | Number from a price list, will "adjust" later |
| Quotes only your middle tier | Didn't read carefully, or fishing |
| Names material grade & mold steel unprompted | Knows their craft |
| "Any price is negotiable" | No engineering behind the number |
| Demands 100% payment up front | Walk. Standard is 30/70 or 50/50 T/T |

## Norms worth knowing

- Sample fees are normal and usually credited against the first order — free samples
  from an unknown factory are a marketing cost someone recoups later.
- Getting 3 quotes isn't about picking the cheapest — it's that three quotes let you
  make each factory explain the other two. The explanations teach you your own product.
- Alibaba/1688 first contact is fine; serious rounds move to email/WeChat with a named
  engineer. If no engineer ever appears, you're talking to a trading company (which can
  be fine — but price it knowing there's a middleman layer).

## Output format

Produce: (1) the filled RFQ in EN or 中文 (or both), (2) the attachment checklist,
(3) a shortlist strategy (how many suppliers, where to find them for this category),
(4) after replies arrive — a comparison table and the follow-up questions per supplier.
